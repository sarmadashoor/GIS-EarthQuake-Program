Jakarta Earthquake 1 oct 2009

6.8 Magnitude

October 1, 2009 12.00 pm GMT
The total number of deaths has increased to 467 in Jakarta, Indonesia, after it was hit by a massive earthquake of magnitude 7.6 on September 30, 2009. The figures of the death tally have been confirmed by the government officials of Indonesia. After experiencing an earthquake of magnitude 7.6, the country again faced a powerful, shallow inland earthquake of magnitude 6.8, today morning. This second earthquake was recorded about 150 miles south of Padang at a depth of less than 20 miles. The tsunami alert which was raised after yesterday's earthquake is now officially revoked.

HALMAHERA, INDONESIA 11 sep 2008

6.6 Magnitude

No damages or casualties have been reported in the earthquake that struck Halmahera, Indonesia on 11 Sept. 2008. The earthquake of magnitude 6.6 on Richter Scale struck the region with a depth of 93 km. Minor earthquakes in the magnitude range of 4.5 to 4.8 are frequent in this region. A strong earthquake of 7.0 had hit Halmahera on May 26, 2003. The epicenter of current quake has been located at 1.865N & 127.438E, which is 119 km north of Ternate, Moluccas, Indonesia, 291 km east of Manado, Sulawesi, Indonesia and 994 km southwest of Koror, Palau
 

SOUTHERN SUMATRA, INDONESIA 8 aug 2008

6.0 Magnitude

An earthquake of magnitude 6.0 in Richter Scale struck Southern Sumatra in Indonesia. The epicenter was located at 3.921S and 101.084E, with a depth of 35 km under the sea. Sumatra Island and the nearby regions are earthquake prone. A powerful earthquake with a magnitude of 8.7 on Richter Scale had hit this region in 2005, bringing serious damage to life (1,300 people died) and property.
